2.5 Powers of a Jordon BlockÂ¶

Consider a \(m\times m\) Jordan block of the form

\[\begin{split} J_m(\lambda) &= \left(\begin{array}{cccccc} \lambda&1&0&\cdots&0&0\\0&\lambda&1&\cdots&0&0\\\vdots&\vdots&\vdots&&\vdots& \vdots\\0&0&0&\cdots&\lambda&1\\0&0&0&\cdots&0&\lambda\end{array}\right), \\ &= \lambda I_m+K, \end{split}\]

where

\[\begin{split} K = \left(\begin{array}{cccccc} 0&1&0&\cdots&0&0\\0&0&1&\cdots&0&0\\\vdots&\vdots&\vdots&&\vdots&\vdots\\0&0&0& \cdots&0&1\\0&0&0&\cdots&0&0\end{array}\right) = \left(\begin{array}{cc}0&I_{m-1}\\0&0\end{array}\right). \end{split}\]

For such a block

Note

Here we have used the Binomial Theorem

\[\begin{split} J_m^r(\lambda) &= (\lambda I_m+K)^r,\\ &= \lambda^rI_m+r\lambda^{r-1}K + \frac{r(r-1)}{2!}\lambda^{r-2}K^2+\cdots K^r,\\ &= \sum_{s=0}^r\binom{r}{s}\lambda^{r-s}K^s. \end{split}\]

Now,

\[\begin{split} K^r =\left(\begin{array}{cc}0&I_{m-r}\\0&0\end{array}\right) \text{ provided } m>r, \end{split}\]

and \(K^r=0\) if \(r\geq m\). Therefore

\[\begin{split} J_m^r(\lambda) &= \sum_{s=0}^{m-1} \binom{r}{s} \lambda^{r-s}K^s,\\ &= \lambda^rI_m+\binom{r}{1}\lambda^{r-1}K+\binom{r}{2}\lambda^{r-2}K^2+\cdots +\binom{r}{m-1}\lambda^{r-m+1}K^{m-1},\\\nonumber &= \lambda^r\left(\begin{array}{cccccc} 1&0&0&\cdots&0&0\\0&1&0&\cdots&0&0\\\vdots&\vdots&\vdots&&\vdots&\vdots\\0&0&0& \cdots&1&0\\0&0&0&\cdots&0&1\end{array}\right)+\binom{r}{1}\lambda^{r-1} \left(\begin{array}{cccccc} 0&1&0&\cdots&0&0\\0&0&1&\cdots&0&0\\\vdots&\vdots&\vdots&&\vdots&\vdots\\0&0&0& \cdots&0&1\\0&0&0&\cdots&0&0\end{array}\right)+\cdots\\ &+\binom{r}{m-1}\lambda^{r-m+1}\left(\begin{array}{cccccc} 0&0&0&\cdots&0&1\\0&0&0&\cdots&0&0\\\vdots&\vdots&\vdots&&\vdots&\vdots\\0&0&0& \cdots&0&0\\0&0&0&\cdots&0&0\end{array}\right),\\ &= \left(\begin{array}{cccccc}\lambda^r & \binom{r}{1}\lambda^{r-1}&\binom{r}{2} \lambda^{r-2}&\ldots&\binom{r}{m-2}\lambda^{r-m+2}&\binom{r}{m-1}\lambda^{r-m+1} \\ 0&\lambda^r&\binom{r}{1}\lambda^{r-1}&\ldots&\binom{r}{m-3}\lambda^{r-m+3} &\binom{r}{m-2}\lambda^{r-m+2}\\ \vdots&\vdots&\vdots&&\vdots&\vdots\\ 0&0&0&\ldots&\lambda^r&\binom{r}{1}\lambda^{r-1}\\ 0&0&0&\ldots&0&\lambda^r \end{array}\right). \end{split}\]

Example 2.5.1 If \(m=5\),

\[\begin{split} J_5^r(\lambda) = \left(\begin{array}{ccccc}\lambda^r&\binom{r}{1}\lambda^{r-1}&\binom{r}{2} \lambda^{r-2}&\binom{r}{3}\lambda^{r-3}&\binom{r}{4}\lambda^{r-4}\\ 0&\lambda^r&\binom{r}{1}\lambda^{r-1}&\binom{r}{2}\lambda^{r-2}&\binom{r}{3} \lambda^{r-3}\\0&0&\lambda^r&\binom{r}{1}\lambda^{r-1}&\binom{r}{2}\lambda^{r-2} \\0&0&0&\lambda^r&\binom{r}{1}\lambda^{r-1}\\ 0&0&0&0&\lambda^r \end{array}\right). \end{split}\]

From the above discussion it follows that:


  1. \(J^r\to 0\) if \(|\lambda|<1\);

  2. \(J^r\) is unbounded if \(|\lambda|>1\);

  3. \(J^r\) is also unbounded if \(\lambda=1\), unless \(m=1\).

Note

This will be important later on when we consider the stability of systems of ODEs and difference equations
